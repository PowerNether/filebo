Picture this: you get a file through Airdrop. Where did your Mac save it? You recorded a Zoom meeting. Where's the video file? You took a screenshot a few minutes ago. How do you find it among all the other files?

Every day, our computers are packed with tons of files we need at different times. Some we make ourselves, while others come from the internet or are sent through chat apps and social media.

When it's time to use a specific file, we've got to remember where we saved it and what it's called. It could be on the desktop, in the Downloads folder, or even Dropbox. It is hard.

That's why we made FileBo – to save you from the headache of searching for these files. FileBo is a pop-up window that shows all the recent files you've created or changed on your Mac. No more searching, just finding!

We made FileBo simple and user-friendly. You can get to your recent files from any app and screen, even in full-screen mode. Just press a hotkey or click the Dock or menubar icon. Pick the option that works best for you.

There's no setup needed. Hit a hotkey and see your Mac's recent files right away. It's that simple.

Track your Cloud documents

Works with Google Docs

FileBo can work with documents and spreadsheets from Google Docs. When creating or modifying any documents, they will be displayed in the FileBo window. This is very convenient, as you don't have to search for the file in the browser.

* To allow FileBo to access your Google Docs, the Google Drive application must be installed on your Mac. Please note that the "Mirror files" option in the application settings must be enabled for proper file synchronization.

Hide all junk

Ignore whats not important

Sometimes you work with files that you don't want to appear in the recent files list. In this case, you can hide them. The Ignore List helps you customize FileBo to display only what you really need and ignore everything else.

You can remove entire folders from your Recent Files list, as well as specific files or all files with a certain extension. You can also group recent files into a parent folder so that they don't take up space in the list.

Simple navigation

Handy sorting,
and file paths.

FileBo offers a file type filter to help you navigate recent files more easily. You can choose to display all recent files, or filter them by images, folders, documents, videos, or audio files.

The date and time under each file will help you determine when it was created. And the file-path in the bottom of the screen will show you where it is located and how much space it occupies on the disk.

Keep the good stuff

See all favorite
files in one click

Each of us has files that we open more often than others. These may include folders with projects, specific applications, or documents.

FileBо offers the ability to show such files in a dedicated "Favorites" panel. Regardless of the application you're using, you can always access these files without having to go through the Finder.

The Favorites panel can contain up to 10 links to your favorite files. You can sort them as you like by holding down the Alt key.

Favorites, Compact or Extended

Choose the view
that works for you

FileBo has three display modes. The first one shows recent files and the Favorites panel. The second one displays only recent files in a compact view. The third one is an expanded view that can show more files.

You can switch between these three modes using the keyboard shortcuts Cmd+1, 2, and 3, respectively.

Can I use FileBo on all my Macs?

You can use FileBo on all of your comuters that are connected to the same Apple ID. If you have enabled Family Sharing, then all members of your family can also access and use FileBo on their own Macs.

Can I try FileBo before buying?

Yes, you can try FileBo for free up for 7 days when you subscribe. If you find that the app doesn't meet your needs, you can cancel the subscription within the first 7 days at no cost. You can also try FileBo in Setapp with the same conditions.

Can I buy instead of subscribing?

We offer a subscription model to make FileBo more budget-friendly, but you also have the option to purchase it at a fixed price and use it as long as you want with live-time support and updates. To access this option, simply click on "More purchase options" in the FileBo Purchase window.

Can FileBo work with external drives?

Currently, FileBo is only compatible with the internal storage of Mac. However, we have plans to include support for external disks, flash drives, and memory cards in the near future.

What macOS are supported?

FileBo relies on a variety of advanced technologies and APIs to deliver top-notch performance and seamless visuals. As a result, FileBo is compatible only with macOS 12 Monterey, macOS 13 Ventura, and newer.

What is FileBo's privacy policy?

FileBo does not collect any personal data. The app utilizes only the built-in features of macOS to locate and display your files in user-friendly way. All files are processed on your Mac locally.

Can FileBo show my Google Docs?

Yes, it can! You should install the Google Drive app to see all your cloud files and documents. Ensure that the syncing option in the app settings is set to "Mirror files."

FileBo will show all documents that you created. If you want to see documents shared with you by others, make sure you add a shortcut of this file to your Google Drive.
